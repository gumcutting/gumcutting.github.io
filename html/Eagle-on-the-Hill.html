Safety Issues and Hazards

Tripping and slipping hazard, terrain is steep in places. Snakes, ants, etc.

Please be sure to turn around at the turn around point(refer to map), then back the vehicle down the bitumen track as there is no turn around point at end of the road,

Cutting techniques

In all sections, the pollarded trees are to be repolladed when the branches are big enough, new trees that have grown to approx. 20cm diameter can be pollarded. Please be sure to pollard branches flush to the stump. 1 or 2 gum cutting staff.

Cutting techniques

Continue to pollard the larger trees when grown to approx. 20 cm diameter. Re pollard pollarded trees when branches are large enough.

1 to 2 gum cutting staff

Seasonal Influences

Being a bitumen track, good year-round cutting

Maintenance Requirements Overview

Stack all large wood and off cuts into piles.

Annual Maintenance

May through to October – burn off and wood and debris piles, Weeding, removal of Watsonia.

Oct/Nov – slash whole site
