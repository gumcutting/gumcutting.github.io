Seasonal Influences
No entry during extreme wet times or extreme heat.

Safety Issues and Hazards
Tripping hazards, working on slopes, carrying gum long distances, hopper ants, snakes etc.
Take care on windy days when dropping the larger straighter trees.

Cutting techniques
Over the last 3 years, Cleland gum cutters have been working pollarding the labelled areas to develop into workable koala feedlot plantations.
At this stage, please leave the large trees as whole until we follow up with how we are cleaning the area up. Red 1: Continue to pollard larger trees to let more light into this area.
Blue 1: Continue to pollard larger trees.
Mixed 1: Pollard koala species in this area, ensuring that the stump has light.
Red 2: Continue to pollard these larger woody trees (a lot of work now, but should be worth it for the regrowth)
Blue Gum 2: Continue to pollard larger trees. Baring in mind if the trees are too close together, pollard the stronger trees and cut the weaker trees off ant ground level to help open up this area.
Mixed 2 and 3: Continue to pollard the larger trees and cutting the smaller weaker trees off at ground level.
Stringybark 1: Continue to pollard the larger trees
Manna Gum 1: Pollard large trees, ensuing that the pollarded stumps get light.
2 gum cutting staff

Maintenance Requirements Overview
All large wood needs to be removed. Larger machinery will be needed. Please see annual maintenance plan.
Red 1: Prune pollarded trees
Blue 1: Prune pollarded trees when ready
Mixed 2 and 3: Prune pollarded trees when ready
A lot of smaller straggly trees can be removed at ground level to open up area for the larger trees and pollarded trees.
Blue 2: Prune pollarded trees when ready
Stringybark 1: Please cut all long Stringybark trunks in to 5 metre lengths in readiness for koala perching.

Please mark these lengths with flagging tape and if possible, stack on the track edge for ease to find on pick up day.
Manna gum 1: leave larger trees complete until we hear if we can get larger machinery in to assist with the clean-up.
