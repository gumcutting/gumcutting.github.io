Volunteer Groups

Between the months of April through to November a Gum Plantation Volunteer group runs one morning a week.
“Many hands help make like work.”
Working closely with Bess Hillyard, Cleland’s Volunteer Coordinator a group of keen volunteers are coordinated to meet one morning a week to assist with weed control, tidying up of debris, pruning, whipper snipping, planting etc.
It generally runs from 9.00am to 1 pm with a morning tea break.
This is a great opportunity to organise small achievable projects in plantations close to Cleland, e.g. Long Ridge.
Projects to work on with the Gum Plantation Volunteers:
Weeding Long Ridge (refer to weed guide for weed ID)
Piling up wood and branches ready for burning off in early spring.
Pruning up pollarded trees to enhance leaf growth
Infill planting in the plantations

Planting day Volunteers.

Each year if in the position to plant new trees Bess can assist in organising volunteers for planting days.
