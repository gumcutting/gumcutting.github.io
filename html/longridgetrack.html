Long Ridge, Cleland Conservation Park

  • Plantation History Planted approx. 1989. Originally designed to be irrigated with the Cleland Wildlife park’s wastewater. Over the years, the irrigation system has not been efficient and has updates along the way. Long Ridge plantation has been broken up into gum sections for ease of identification and management. Seasonal Influences

Good safe year-round cutting. Solid track. Long Ridge plantation is saved for extreme weather conditions cutting when too dangerous to go to any other locations. It can also be used when the team is running short of time or short-staffed. Keeping staff may need to access Long Ridge if needing extra special gum for unwell koalas.

Cutting techniques

In all sections, the pollarded trees are to be repolladed when the branches are big enough, new trees that have grown to approx. 20cm diameter can be pollarded. Please be sure to pollard branches flush to the stump. 1 or 2 gum cutting staff.

Cutting techniques

Please pile all offcuts in neat piles on track edge. If too large try and pile in the plantation and then please let the gum coordinator know that there is a clean up with the plantation trees.

Annual Maintenance

February – Blackberry spraying (need to check with Bess) April through to November- hand weeding, drill and fill of olives May through to November – burning of the wood and debris piles October/November – slashing of whole site, whipper snipping amongst trees where slasher cannot access.

Red Gum 1, Long Ridge

Plantation History Planted 1989
This area has never thrived.

Cutting techniques

Trees have been struggling for many years. Stage 1, cut all struggling trees back to ground level, to see if they reshoot better.
Stage 2, if these recut trees do not do well, take them out completely and plant Stringybark.
1 to 2 gum cutting staff

Please stack all off cuts neatly on track edge.
